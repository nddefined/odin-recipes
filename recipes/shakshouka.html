Shakshouka

Description

This is a recipe for shakshouka, a Maghrebi dish of eggs poached in a sauce of tomatoes, olive oil, peppers, onion, garlic, and various spices.

Shakshouka is popular throughout North Africa and the Middle East. The name of the dish means "mixture" in Maghrebi Arabic. Variations of the dish can be found in various cuisines.

Ingredients

  • 6 medium or large eggs
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 0.5 tablespoon crushed garlic
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 0.25 cup feta cheese
  • 1 red jalapeno, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cumin, cayenne, and paprika, to taste
  • Cilantro, for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and add onions and garlic. Saute until onions and garlic become translucent.
  2. Add peppers and spices and saute for another 5 minutes.
  3. Add tomatoes, salt, and pepper to sauce and cook for another 10 minutes over medium-low heat.
  4. Using a spoon, make six welts in sauce and drop an egg into each welt.
  5. Place pan in oven and bake for about 10 minutes at 360 degrees F, or until egg whites are no longer translucent.
  6. Garnish dish with feta cheese and chopped cilantro. Serve.
