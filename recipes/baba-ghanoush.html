Baba Ghanoush

Description

This is a recipe for baba ghanoush, a Levantine dip of cooked eggplant, olive oil, lemon juice, tahini, and various seasonings.

Baba ghanoush is eaten widely throughout the Levant. It is a nutritious dish, high in fiber and antioxidants, and contains heart-healthy fats and various vitamins and minerals.

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1.5 tablespoons tahini sauce
  • 0.5 medium lemon, juiced
  • 0.5 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pinch dried parsley flakes

Steps

  1. Gather ingredients. Place one rack in the lower third of the oven and another rack in the upper third. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F, or 200 degrees C.
  2. Cut shallow slit along side of eggplant and place into a baking dish.
  3. Roast on lower rack of preheated oven until eggplant is completely shrunken and soft, about 40 minutes. Move to upper rack and continue baking until skin is charred, about 5 more minutes. Remove from oven and let sit until cool enough to handle, 15 to 20 minutes.
  4. Peel and discard eggplant skin. Place eggplant flesh into bowl; stir in garlic, tahini, lemon juice, pepper flakes, and salt until combined.
  5. Drizzle olive oil over top and garnish with parsley.
